EXPEDIA (Summer Internship)

In the summer of 2016, I interned at Expedia Inc as an Interaction Design intern working on their Hotels shopping experience

INTRODUCTION

Expedia’s website lets users shop for hotel rooms from a choice of multiple hotels based on the destination. However, when users require two or more rooms, they cannot pick and choose rooms of their choice or customize their bed types. During my Summer (2016) internship at Expedia, I was tasked with exploring ways to provide a better hotel shopping experience by letting users customize their beds and occupancy configurations while booking hotel rooms.

Role

I was the only person working on this project. I was involved in the following activities:

  • Research
  • Interaction Design
  • Visual Design

Problem & Goals

The problem

When booking multiple hotel rooms for a large group of guests, users cannot choose different room of their choice within a hotel. Also, they cannot not accommodate their group into multiple rooms based on their needs and preference. When shopping for multiple rooms, they are limited to a small set of choices provided by the website.

Project Goals

I was asked to come up with design recommendations to solve the beds and occupancy problem. After having an initial discussion with the stakeholders about the problem and the requirements, I shortlisted the following goals for the project:

  • Help users shop for different hotel rooms and rates in the same booking session.
  • Provide a clear understanding of available bed types within the room(s) for users when booking hotel rooms.
  • Provide an ability to filter and choose rooms with different bed types.
  • Improve the hotel shopping experience.

Research

Literature survey

To understand the problem space better, I commenced the project by doing literature survey of the studies Expedia had conducted in their labs. Some of the key findings of the literature survey are:

  • On an average 6 out of 10 people chose 2 or more rooms when shopping for hotel rooms for themselves and their family & friends
  • Users want to be able to search or ﬁlter based on their preferred bed type
  • Bed type is the highest reason for customer calls

Competitve Teardown Analysis

Along with literature survey, I also did competitive tear-downs of other online travel agencies to understand the methods employed by them to solve the same problem.

Research Questions

The top research questions that I decided to focus on after the research are:

  1. How can users select multiple rooms and rates in the Hotel info page?
  2. What is the best place to distribute guests, Homepage or Hotel info page?
  3. How can users customize their rooms with different bed types?

I quickly tested these wireframes with my colleagues and website users to get their feedback on the process flow, learn about how they would go about booking a hotel room for a group and uncover usability issues. Some of the feedback I received are:

  • People weren’t clear of the sequential room selection pattern. They wanted to pick multiple rooms at the same time.
  • Providing ways to add multiple quantities of rooms of same rate.
  • Being able to view playback of choices made.
  • Accessibility issues with the sticky elements - header and checkout button.
 

Design

After exploring multiple variants and possible flows, I came up with a final design recommendation that made use of multiple patterns I had explored.

Takeaways

Working with a team of very smart and talented designers was a great experience for me. This was the first time I was working with a design team and a product this large and I was able to understand the nuances of working in such conditions. Some of my takeaways are:

  • Focusing on the problem and the users while designing.
  • Creating multiple variants of the same design to explore different design possibilities and comparing them with each other to find the most appropriate solution.
  • Asking yourself a lot of ‘Why’s while designing helps get to the problem faster and stay focused on the same.
  • Attention to detail - Thinking about the purpose behind a design, how it would affect a user's decision making process. Design should help reduce confusion in users' minds and help them get to their goals faster.
  • Designing for accessibility - Accessibility is a huge factor while designing interactions. All possible users and their limitations must be considered & the design should help everyone achieve their goals equally easily.

What I would do differently

  • Work with a team of at least 2 or 3 members so that the research phase can be done faster and generate a broader set of ideas. Being the only person in the team, it was a challenge to handle all the processes of UX for a project of this magnitude.
  • Get a better understanding of the business and accessibility limitations before designing. In this project there were certain design patterns that had accessibility concerns due to which I had to redesign some elements and explore different patterns.
