Periodical.ly

Periodical.ly is a web service that provides low-cost and easy access to articles from variety of popular online magazines and publications.

INTRODUCTION

Peridical.ly is a web service that lets reading enthusiasts buy and read articles online without having to frequently encounter multiple paywalls and login forms. With a single log-in, Periodical.ly helps users to bypass paywalls and provides seamless access to their favorite articles and web content. With a unique credit system, Periodical.ly’s goal is to eliminate the friction between a reader and the publishers by getting rid of the paywall.

Most premium online magazines have restricted access to the amount of content people can read for free. With a surge in competition in quantity and quality by free content providers, publishers are finding it hard to push across their content to online readers.

For the users, they get turned off by the paywalls that prevent them from accessing premium content. Paywalls are digital walls that prevent users from accessing online content without a paid subscription. Also, readers would hesitate to purchase an entire magazine subscription just to read a single article that caught their attention.

Existing Model

To find out the cost-per-credit and the we did a survey of 8 different magazines from the US and UK. Magazines do not sell articles individually and they use an monthly/quarterly/yearly subscription models. By looking at the cost per issue and the number of articles each magazine carries, we were able to find out the cost per article. From the research we discovered that premium magazines like What Mobile, National Geographic and Empire charge as much as 60¢ while magazines like The Forbes and The Macworld charge approximately 30¢ per article in their magazines. The trend showed that cost/article varied between 10¢ and 60¢.

Periodical.ly Model

Looking at the trend, we figured out that each credit can be priced at 33¢ and the articles are priced between 1 and 3 credits. Pricing is dynamic and can be varied depending on the author, publication and popularity of the article.

Users can purchase a minimum of 3 credits for $1 and higher based on multiple plans from their Periodical.ly dashboard.

PIVOT

Although the idea of subscribing to magazines, newspapers and other publications along with purchasing single-issue articles was well received, due to time constraints we took a step back to focus on the idea we initially started with - Making single issue articles easily accessible for readers. With the sharpened focus we iterated on our exitsting wireframes and designs and made the app exclusively for purchasing single-issue articles from various digital publications.

FINAL DESIGN

Findings

There were some important takeaways from this project:

  • I learned how to develop a business model for a viable product using the business model canvas.
  • It's important to not get tempted by feature creep. Our pivot helped me realise that it's very important to stay focused on the idea and the 'MVP'.
  • Learned how to develop a business model for a viable product using the business model canvas.
  • Customer delight and perspectives are most important. Always design with the end user and his conveniences/annoyances in mind.

Future

Some of the future considerations for the project are:
