DATESCOUT

Datescout is a dating app deisgned to connect people based on their mutual interests

Page renders best on Chrome browser. Mobile optimization in progress.

Introduction

Datescout is an activity centric dating app that connects people based on their interests and activities they are doing.

This app was designed and prototyped as a part of my Interaction Design course. This was an individual project.

Design process

The design process involved:

  • Initial research
  • Sketching
  • Visual Design
  • Interaction Design

Design Language

For this app I used Google’s material design as the design language and used patterns suggested by the Material design guidelines.

Time taken

2 weeks.

Tools used

Sketch - Designing

Framer - Prototyping

Team

I was the only person working on this project.

Persona

To help me guide through the design, I created a persona of Mark Andrews, a 27 year old professional who is looking to find a date.

Evolution Of The Idea

The idea first started out as a map-centric dating app where people would carry out activities that makes them unique and add them on a map. The app would then recommend other people doing similar activities. However after getting feedback from my peers, I learned that people were less open to the map centric idea and I iterated it to a more activity centric idea.

Site map

After I knew which direction I would be heading with the idea, and who I'm building it for, I created a site map to help me stay focused on what pages to create and build the navigational flow structure.

Onboarding

  1. Sign-up using facebook.
  2. Click on the bio box to add bio.
  3. Mark is interested in dating females. Click on the females switch.
  4. Click on the location field to add location
  5. Proceed to choose interests
  6. Pick Traveling, Entrepreneurship and Technology (in that order) as interests.
  7. Proceed to start scouting.

Scouting

  1. The app prompts Mark to add his first picture. Skip it for now.
  2. In the feed, click on Kate’s profile to visit her profile.
  3. Click on Kate’s black and white picture enlarge. (The horizontal ‘cover-flow’ swipe interactions for pictures do not work)
  4. Explore Kate’s ‘Common’ and ‘About’ page.
  5. Go to Kate’s ‘Activity’ tab.
  6. Click on the puppy’s picture to enlarge the picture. (The horizontal ‘cover-flow’ swipe interactions for pictures do not work)
  7. Return to kate’s profile page.
  8. Press the ‘Heart’ button to indicate interest in matching with her. Assume there is a delay in Kate viewing Mark’s profile and pressing the ‘Heart’ button on her app
  9. When kate presses the heart FAB on her app, Mark gets a notification on the navbar. Click on the pink Heart icon.
  10. There is a new chat request by Kate.
  11. Click on her chathead to view chat and continue conversation. Assume the conversation has been started
  12. Return to home page from the chat page.

Challenges faced

Some of the main challenges I faced during this project:

1. Choosing the right navigation pattern - Each screen presented different information. It took me a while to study and implement navigation structure across every page that would not be comfusing to the users.

2. Choosing the right platform to build the prototype - I tried to create the prototype using Axure, Flinto and Framer before settling with Framer as code-based tool gave me the most flexibility to prototype. However the tool soon got very resource intensive and the process got very slow, and I had to eventually compromise on complicated interactions.

3. Creating animations - I wanted to explore some level of animations for transitions and other interactions where animations help in communicating the content better (Eg: Pictures expanding into full screen). Unfortunately due to the lack of time, I wasn't able to explore this part.

Reflections

During this exercise I got some much needed hand-on experince in designing interactions for a mobile app and the dating app context was a very interesting one as I don’t use a dating app. Before designing, I had to learn how dating apps work, read about popular dating apps and their evolution over time to get a better understanding of the context. With the limited time I had, I could not explore all the possible interactions and screen beyond what is possible in the prototype. On the whole, I wanted to explore new ways to create a dating app and I am happy I was able to create this prototype. In the further interations I would like to:

1. Have a detailed profile set-up page and integration with other social media profiles.

2. Explore other tools for prototyping animations, transitions and interactions to provide better affrodances. This is something I would like to work more on. .

3. Consider more enagagement ideas - Additional ways to keep users hooked to the app and get more incentives for staying on it.
